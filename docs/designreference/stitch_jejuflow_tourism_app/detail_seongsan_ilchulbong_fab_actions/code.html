Natural Wonder of Jeju

Formed by hydrovolcanic eruptions upon a shallow seabed about 5,000 years ago, Seongsan Ilchulbong rises 182 meters above sea level. This dramatic tuff cone is world-renowned for its perfectly preserved bowl-like crater and breathtaking views of the sunrise over the East Sea.

The climb to the summit consists of a well-maintained wooden stairway that weaves through unique rock formations, offering constant views of the surrounding Udo Island and the emerald waters that Jeju is famous for.
